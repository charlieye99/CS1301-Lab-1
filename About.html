The saxophone was created by Belgian instrument maker Adolphe Sax in 1840. Although they are made mostly from brass, they are considered woodwinds as they use a single-reed mouthpiece.

Although there are many different types of saxophones, the most commonly played are the soprano, alto, tenor, and baritone. Most players begin learning with the alto saxophone, depicted here.

Saxophones are used in a variety of music, from classical to jazz to songs on the radio today. This is because they have a unique sound, but can also imitate other instruments.
