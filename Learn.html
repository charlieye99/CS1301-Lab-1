Choosing a Saxophone

The first thing you need to consider is what kind of saxophone you want to start with! Most players begin learning with the alto, but you can read below to find out what saxophone is right for you.

An important detail to note is the different models of saxophones you can buy, ranging from beginner to intermediate to professional. If you're just starting off, a beginner saxophone is the best option for you!

The advantages of buying intermediate/professional models are durability, quality of sound, and obviously aesthetics.

Although you can buy saxophones online, your best bet is to find a local store and try playing them before you buy! Each different model of saxophone has subtle but sometimes drastic differences in sound and feel.

If you're really looking to save money, you can buy a used saxophone. If you make sure you test them before purchase, you can save some serious money.

Alto

The alto is the saxophone most learn to play on first, and the most popular. Because of its medium size, smaller mouthpiece, and lower air requirement, it's great for developing basic techniques and skills.

Alto players have an extremely large amount of music available to them, especially for classical. They are often used in jazz as well.

Beginner Model:

$600-1200

Intermediate Model: $1500-2500

Professional Model: $3000-5000

 Charlie Parker

Tenor

The tenor is probably the first thing that comes to mind when you think about saxophones. It is slightly larger, requiring more air to play, so it is more of a challenge to learn at first, but has a richer and deeper tone.

If you're looking to play a lot of jazz, this is the saxophone for you. It produces the most iconic saxophone jazz sound, and is also the type you hear in most songs on the radio.

Beginner Model: $1000-2000

Intermediate Model: $2000-3000

Professional Model: $4000-6000

 Cannonball Adderley

Baritone

TThe baritone is the lowest-pitched saxophone, also being largest and requiring the most air. It is tougher to learn than the others.

Baritone is used in both classical and jazz, acting as a bass voice, but it's home is in R&B and rock.

Intermediate Model: $2000-4000

Professional Model: $6000-8000

 Gerry Mulligan

Soprano

The soprano is the highest pitched and smallest of the saxophones. Although it tends to be cheaper, it is hard to master because of these qualities, as it is hard to control the higher register.

The soprano finds its place in orchestras and classical music, because of its rich tone in the higher registers, but is sometimes played in jazz.

Intermediate Model: $800-1500

Professional Model: $2000-4000

 John Coltrane
